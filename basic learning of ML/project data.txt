 Global waste generation is expected to increase by 70% by 2050 compared to 2016 levels, reaching 3.4 billion tonnes annually.
 Waste generation per capita is also on the rise, with an average global increase of 19% projected by 2050. (Source: World Bank)
High-income nations currently generate about 34% of the world's waste, but low- and middle-income countries are expected to see a much faster increase in waste generation in the coming decades. (Source: World Bank)

The global average recycling rate for municipal solid waste (MSW) is estimated to be around 17%. (Source: World Bank)
The most recent national survey on municipal waste management in Bangladesh was conducted in 2021. It found that only 3.15% of the 80 lakh tonnes of MSW generated by municipalities across the country were recycled.

Estimates for the formal recycling market in Bangladesh vary, but it's likely in the range of USD 150-250 million annually. This includes revenue generated by registered recyclers and processors of materials like plastic, paper, and metal.(formal sector)
The informal sector plays a significant role in Bangladesh's recycling industry, particularly for materials like plastic and metal. It's estimated that the informal sector's contribution to the recycling market could be anywhere from USD 200-400 million annually.
